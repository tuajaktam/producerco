Why Rice?

The crop that feeds the world is also one of its largest climate problems.

Rice paddies are anaerobic environments. When flooded continuously — as most traditional rice cultivation does — they generate methane at scale. Rice agriculture contributes roughly 10% of total global agricultural greenhouse gas emissions, making it one of the largest single sources of food-system methane.

At the same time, rice is the primary caloric foundation for more than half the world's population. Eliminating rice cultivation is not an option. Transforming how it is grown, verified, and traded is.

Producer Co. was built on this insight: the practices that reduce rice's climate footprint — Alternate Wetting & Drying, precision input management, soil health monitoring — are already known. What has been missing is the digital infrastructure to measure them at scale, verify them credibly, and connect that verification to the markets willing to pay for it.

Core Methodology

How Alternate Wetting & Drying (AWD) works.

AWD is the cornerstone sustainable rice practice — a water management technique that dramatically reduces methane emissions while often improving yield. Producer Co. digitises and verifies every step.

01
💧
Flood Field

Field is flooded to standard depth for transplanting and early growth, as in conventional practice.

02
📉
Allow to Drain

Water is allowed to drain naturally. IoT sensors and satellite imagery monitor soil moisture levels continuously.

03
📡
Monitor & Verify

Producer Co.'s dMRV system logs the drying cycle, timestamps it, and cross-references with methane flux readings.

04
🔄
Reflood at Threshold

Fields are reflooded when soil moisture reaches a safe threshold — protecting yield while breaking the anaerobic methane cycle.

05
✅
Issue Carbon Credit

Verified cycles are aggregated into a season-long emissions reduction report. Third-party sign-off triggers carbon credit issuance.
